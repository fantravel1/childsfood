Creamy Hummus Bowl

A dip that builds confidence. Kids control every bite.

Ingredients

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained
  • 3 tablespoons tahini
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2-3 tablespoons water
  • Salt and pepper
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 2 carrots, cut into sticks
  • 1 bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 cucumber, sliced
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes
  • Whole wheat pita bread
  • Paprika for garnish

Instructions

  1. 1
    Roast the Chickpeas

    Preheat oven to 400¬∞F. Toss drained chickpeas with olive oil, salt, and paprika. Spread on a baking sheet and roast for 10-12 minutes until crispy.

  2. 2
    Blend the Hummus

    In a food processor, combine tahini, lemon juice, garlic, and chickpeas. Blend until smooth. Add water gradually until creamy.

  3. 3
    Prep the Dippers

    Cut carrots, peppers, and cucumber into kid-friendly sticks and slices. Halve cherry tomatoes.

  4. 4
    Build the Bowl

    Spread hummus in a wide bowl. Top with roasted chickpeas and a drizzle of olive oil. Arrange veggie dippers all around. Let kids dip!

üé® Make It Theirs

  • Dipping Contest: Who can dip the straightest line of veggies in their hummus?
  • Color Challenge: "Can you eat one veggie of every color?"
  • Flavor Swaps: Try paprika, sumac, or zaatar sprinkled on top for different flavors
  • Mix-In Option: Add a spoonful of tahini directly to their hummus bowl
  • Create a Pattern: Let kids arrange veggie dippers in rainbow patterns around the bowl

üåü Why Kids Love This

Hummus is a gateway food. It's creamy, mild, and satisfying. The roasted chickpeas add a crunchy, fun texture that keeps little hands busy. And because kids are controlling the dipping, they feel like they're in charge.

Nutritionally: Chickpeas are packed with plant-based protein and fiber. Tahini adds healthy fats for brain development. The variety of colorful veggies means kids are exploring different textures and flavors at their own pace.

üí° Skill Building

Ages 3-4: Let them wash the vegetables and dip into the hummus with your help.

Ages 5-6: They can slice softer vegetables (cucumber, bell pepper) with a plastic knife and help arrange the dippers.

Ages 7+: They can help make the hummus in the food processor, juice the lemon, and create the whole bowl independently.
