Jollof Rice Party

The rice that brings families to the table. Golden, aromatic, and loved across Africa.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 large onion, finely diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 cups long-grain white rice
  • 1 can diced tomatoes (14 oz)
  • 1 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 teaspoon tomato paste
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • Salt and pepper
  • Fresh cilantro

Instructions

  1. 1
    Saut√© Aromatics

    Heat oil in a large pot. Add diced onion and cook 3 minutes until softened. Add garlic and cook 1 more minute.

  2. 2
    Toast the Rice

    Add rice and stir for 2 minutes. You'll see the rice become slightly translucent at the edges.

  3. 3
    Add Tomatoes & Broth

    Pour in diced tomatoes, tomato paste, broth, paprika, and bay leaf. Stir well and bring to a boil.

  4. 4
    Simmer with Vegetables

    Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer 20-25 minutes. Add bell pepper and mixed vegetables in the last 10 minutes.

  5. 5
    Fluff and Celebrate

    Remove from heat, let sit 5 minutes, then fluff with a fork. Garnish with cilantro. Watch the golden-red color transform!

üé® Make It Theirs

  • The Great Jollof Debate: In West Africa, every country claims they make the best jollof. Let your child vote on the flavor!
  • Veggie Detective: "Can you spot all 3 types of vegetables hiding in the rice?"
  • Color Mixing: Watch how the red tomato sauce turns the rice golden-orange
  • Texture Contrast: Pair with crunchy veggie slaw or protein source for balance
  • Spice Level Control: Make a mild version and slowly increase paprika as they grow comfortable

üåü Why Kids Love This

Jollof rice is a celebration on a plate. The golden-red color is visually exciting, and kids love the fragrant, slightly sweet tomato flavor. It's a one-pot meal that feels like a party.

Culturally meaningful: This is authentic West African cuisine‚Äînot simplified, but kid-friendly. You're introducing your child to real food traditions from Nigeria, Ghana, Senegal, and beyond.
