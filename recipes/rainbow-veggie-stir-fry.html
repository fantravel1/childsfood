🥗 Ingredients

Vegetables

  • 1 pepperRed bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 pepperYellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 cupBroccoli florets, bite-sized
  • 1 cupSugar snap peas, trimmed
  • 2 mediumCarrots, julienned or coin-sliced
  • 1 cupPurple cabbage, shredded

Sauce

  • 2 tbspLow-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tbspHoney or maple syrup
  • 1 tspFresh ginger, grated
  • 1 cloveGarlic, minced
  • 1 tspRice vinegar
  • 2 tbspSesame oil

Base

  • 2 cupsCooked jasmine rice (or noodles/quinoa)

📋 Step-by-Step Instructions

1

Prep the Rainbow

Wash and cut all vegetables into kid-friendly sizes. Slice bell peppers into strips, break broccoli into small florets, trim snap peas, julienne or coin-slice carrots, and shred the cabbage. Kids can help wash, tear, and sort by color!

⏱ 7 min
2

Mix the Sauce

In a small bowl, whisk together soy sauce, honey (or maple syrup), grated ginger, minced garlic, and rice vinegar until smooth. Taste it — adjust sweetness if your child prefers it milder.

⏱ 2 min
3

Heat the Wok

Heat sesame oil in a large wok or skillet over medium-high heat until the oil shimmers and just begins to smoke. A hot pan means crispy, vibrant veggies.

⏱ 1 min
4

Cook the Hard Veggies First

Add carrots and broccoli to the hot wok. Stir-fry for 3 minutes, tossing frequently, until they begin to soften but stay beautifully crunchy. The colors will brighten.

⏱ 3 min
5

Add the Rainbow

Toss in bell peppers, snap peas, and purple cabbage. Stir-fry for another 2–3 minutes. Watch the colors come alive — this is where kids love to peek into the pan!

⏱ 3 min
6

Sauce It Up

Pour the sauce over the sizzling vegetables and toss everything together for about 1 minute until every piece is coated and glossy. The kitchen will smell incredible.

⏱ 1 min
7

Serve Your Rainbow

Scoop warm rice, noodles, or quinoa into bowls. Top with the stir-fry and let kids arrange their plate to look like a rainbow, a face, a garden — whatever inspires them!

⏱ 2 min

💡 Tips & Variations

🧂 Flavor Bridge for Picky Eaters

If your child is hesitant about new veggies, start with just the ones they know and add one new color each time. The sweet sauce acts as a "flavor bridge" — the familiar sweetness makes new textures less scary.

🥗 Texture Tip

For younger kids who prefer softer textures, cook the veggies 1–2 minutes longer. For crunch-lovers, keep stir-fry time short. Let them choose their texture preference!

🌿 Make It Vegan

Swap honey for maple syrup and skip the shrimp/chicken protein option. The recipe is naturally plant-based and just as delicious without animal products.

📦 Meal Prep Friendly

This stir-fry keeps well in the fridge for 3–4 days. Pack it in bento boxes for school lunches. The veggies stay colorful and the sauce actually gets tastier overnight.
