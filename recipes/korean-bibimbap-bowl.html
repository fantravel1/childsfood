Kid-Friendly Bibimbap Bowl

A rainbow of vegetables that kids mix together. The ultimate interactive meal!

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked short-grain rice
  • 4 eggs
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 2 carrots, julienned
  • 1 zucchini, julienned
  • 1 cup mushrooms, sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber, julienned
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon mild gochujang (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons mayonnaise
  • Toasted sesame seeds
  • Green onions, sliced

Instructions

  1. 1
    Cook Rice

    Prepare short-grain rice and divide into bowls.

  2. 2
    Prep Veggies

    Julienne carrots, zucchini, and cucumber. Slice peppers and mushrooms. Keep colorful and separate.

  3. 3
    Saut√© Spinach

    Blanch spinach for 1 minute, then saut√© with sesame oil and soy sauce.

  4. 4
    Cook Other Veggies

    Saut√© carrots, zucchini, and mushrooms briefly until tender-crisp. Keep them colorful!

  5. 5
    Fry Eggs

    Fry eggs so yolks are still slightly runny. The runny yolk becomes the sauce!

  6. 6
    Assemble & Mix

    Arrange veggies on rice in groups. Top with egg. Let kids mix everything together!

  • Color Rainbow: Arrange the bowl like a colorful palette with each veggie in its own section

  • Veggie Choice: Which vegetables go in their bowl? They decide!

üåü Why Kids Love This

Bibimbap means "mixed rice." Kids LOVE mixing food themselves. Every spoonful is different, and they're in complete control. The runny egg yolk becomes a creamy sauce that brings everything together.

Visual Delight: The colorful vegetables arranged like a painter's palette is exciting for young eyes. Plus, they can pick and choose which veggies they eat.
