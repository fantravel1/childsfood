Black Bean Quesadilla

Crispy on the outside, creamy on the inside. Quesadillas are a kid favorite.

Ingredients

  • 1 can black beans, drained
  • 1 cup shredded cheddar cheese
  • 1/2 red bell pepper, diced
  • 1/4 cup fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin
  • 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper
  • 8 flour tortillas (6-inch)
  • 1 tablespoon butter or oil
  • Sour cream & salsa

Instructions

  1. 1
    Prepare the Filling

    In a bowl, lightly mash black beans (keep some texture). Mix in cheese, bell pepper, cilantro, cumin, garlic powder, salt, and pepper.

  2. 2
    Assemble Quesadillas

    Place a tortilla down. Spread 1/4 of the filling on half of it. Fold the other half over. Repeat with remaining tortillas.

  3. 3
    Cook Until Golden

    Heat butter in a skillet over medium heat. Place a quesadilla in the pan and cook 3-4 minutes until golden. Flip and cook 2-3 minutes more until cheese melts.

  4. 4
    Serve with Toppings

    Cut into triangles. Serve with sour cream, salsa, and lime. Let kids customize their dipping sauce!

  • Mix & Match Filling: Kids choose: cilantro or no cilantro? Extra cheese or light? Spicy salsa or mild?
  • Fold Technique: Let them practice folding the tortilla into a perfect half-moon
  • Flip Challenge: Who can flip the quesadilla without it breaking? (With supervision!)
  • Sauce Scientist: Blend different salsas, sour cream, and guacamole to create a signature dip
  • Shape & Slice: Cut into triangles, squares, or strips‚Äîwhatever they prefer

üåü Why Kids Love This

Quesadillas are a texture dream. Crispy outside, gooey inside, soft beans, and a little crunch from bell pepper. Plus, kids feel like they're eating "Mexican food" which feels special and adventurous.

Budget & Nutrition: Black beans are packed with protein and fiber. This meal costs under $3 per serving and is filling enough for a full lunch or light dinner.
