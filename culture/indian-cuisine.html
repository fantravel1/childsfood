🇮🇳 Indian Cuisine

A world of spices, regional diversity, and thousands of years of culinary wisdom. From the Himalayas to the coasts.

The Magic of Spices

Indian cooking is built on spice knowledge. Every spice serves a purpose:

Turmeric (Haldi): Golden, earthy, anti-inflammatory. The color and heart of many dishes.
Cumin (Jeera): Warm, earthy, aids digestion. A foundation spice.
Coriander (Dhanya): Fresh, citrusy when raw; warm when cooked.
Ginger (Adrak): Warm, spicy, healing. Used fresh, dried, and powdered.
Garam Masala: The soul blend—cinnamon, cardamom, cloves, bay leaves. Each region has its own blend.

Regional Diversity

  • North Indian: Breads (naan, roti), cream-based curries, tandoori
  • South Indian: Rice-based, coconut-heavy, spicier, dosas, idlis
  • East Indian: Fish, mustard oil, sweet flavors
  • West Indian: Peanuts, sesame, coastal seafood

Introducing Kids to Indian Food

Start with mild spice levels. Indian food teaches children that spices don't have to be hot—they can be warm, aromatic, and delicious. Blooming spices in oil releases their gentler flavors.

Celebrate the layers. Every dish has multiple flavors that unfold. This teaches taste exploration.
