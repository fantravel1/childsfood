The Basic Process

Step 1: Choose Your Meals (Sunday)

Pick 4-6 dinners for the week. Include one repeat meal (Wednesday = same as Tuesday, just different side). Look at your calendar‚Äîbusy nights need quick meals.

Step 2: Write Your Grocery List (Monday)

Check what you have at home. Only buy what's missing. Group list by store layout (produce, protein, pantry).

Step 3: Batch Prep (Sunday)

Chop vegetables, cook grains, marinate proteins. 1 hour of prep = effortless dinners all week.

Step 4: Cook Efficiently (Throughout Week)

Use prep work. Roast vegetables once, use in multiple meals. Cook extra rice for next day's fried rice.

Reducing Food Waste

  • ‚úÖ Leafy Greens: Wash & store properly in containers = they last twice as long
  • ‚úÖ Root Vegetables: Store in cool, dark place. Last weeks.
  • ‚úÖ Ripe Fruit: Freeze bananas, berries for smoothies
  • ‚úÖ Vegetable Scraps: Freeze for homemade broth
  • ‚úÖ Stale Bread: Make breadcrumbs, croutons, or bread pudding
  • ‚úÖ Leftover Rice: Fried rice, rice pudding, soup base
  • ‚úÖ Small Amounts of Meat: Add to soups, pasta, rice dishes

Freezer Basics

Your freezer is a time-saving, money-saving tool:

  • Freeze extra portions of every meal for future quick dinners
  • Make & freeze soups in bulk
  • Prep vegetables once, freeze in portions
  • Batch cook ground meat, divide into portions, freeze
  • Label everything with date (use within 3 months for best quality)

Getting Kids Involved

Let them help with meal planning. Ask: "What's your favorite meal?" "What vegetables do you want to try?" Buy those things. When they choose the meal, they're more excited to eat it. Win-win.
