The Big 8 Allergens

These account for ~90% of food allergic reactions in children:

ü•ú Peanuts

Legume (not tree nut). Found in obvious places but also sauces, candy, baked goods.

üå≥ Tree Nuts

Almonds, walnuts, cashews, pecans, etc. Often in granola, nut butters, baked goods.

ü•õ Dairy

Milk, cheese, yogurt, butter, whey, casein. Hidden in baked goods and processed foods.

üåæ Gluten

Wheat, barley, rye. Found in bread, pasta, cereals, sauces. Cross-contamination risk high.

ü•ö Eggs

In baked goods, mayonnaise, pastas, some sauces. Sometimes hidden as "lecithin."

üå± Soy

Soy sauce, soybean oil, tofu. Sneaks into sauces, broths, and processed foods.

ü¶ê Shellfish

Shrimp, crab, lobster, mussels. High risk for cross-contamination in kitchens.

üêü Fish

Salmon, cod, tuna. Often in Asian dishes, sauces, and broths (check label!).

Critical Safety Practices

üìñ READ EVERY LABEL

Manufacturers change recipes. A "safe" food last month may not be safe today. Check ingredients, allergen warnings, and manufacturing facility warnings.

üßº PREVENT CROSS-CONTAMINATION

Separate utensils, cutting boards, and preparation areas. Use dedicated toaster, colander, condiment jars for allergic child if living with others who eat allergen.

‚ö†Ô∏è COMMUNICATE WITH SCHOOLS/CAREGIVERS

Written allergy plan, photos of safe foods, emergency contacts, prescribed medications (epinephrine auto-injectors if needed).

üëÅÔ∏è WATCH FOR SYMPTOMS

Mild: Itchy mouth, swelling lips/tongue. Severe: Difficulty breathing, anaphylaxis. Know your child's symptoms.

When to Call 911

  • Difficulty breathing or wheezing
  • Throat tightness or hoarseness
  • Swelling of face, lips, or tongue
  • Vomiting or severe abdominal pain
  • Dizziness or loss of consciousness
  • Any sign of anaphylaxis

Do not wait. Call immediately. Use epinephrine auto-injector if prescribed, then call 911.
