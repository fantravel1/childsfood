Living gluten-free with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity.

What is Gluten?

Gluten is a protein found in wheat, barley, and rye. For people with celiac disease, it triggers an immune response that damages the small intestine. Even trace amounts (20ppm) matter.

Obvious Gluten Sources

  • Bread, pasta, cereals (wheat, barley, rye)
  • Baked goods, cookies, cakes
  • Most sauces (flour-based thickener)
  • Beer (contains barley)
  • Some soups (flour-thickened)

Hidden Gluten (Always Read Labels!)

  • Soy sauce (contains wheatâ€”use tamari)
  • Malt flavoring (from barley)
  • Modified food starch (may contain wheat)
  • Deli meats (may have fillers)
  • Salad dressings & marinades (flour thickening)
  • Spice blends (anti-caking agents)
  • Some sauces labeled "gluten-free" still have cross-contamination risk

Cross-Contamination Prevention

This is CRITICAL for celiac disease:

  • âœ… Dedicated toaster (not shared with gluten bread)
  • âœ… Separate cutting boards, colander, serving utensils
  • âœ… Clean counters thoroughly (breadcrumbs spread easily)
  • âœ… Wash hands after handling gluten foods
  • âœ… Use dedicated jars for spreads (no crumbs from regular bread knife)
  • âœ… Separate sponge & dish cloth
  • âœ… Label foods clearly if sharing a kitchen

Restaurant & Social Eating

Always inform servers about celiac disease. Ask about:

  • How foods are prepared (shared equipment?)
  • Sauces & marinades (flour-thickened?)
  • Spice blends (anti-caking agents?)
  • Cross-contamination procedures
